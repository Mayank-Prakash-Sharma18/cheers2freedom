## Content Management System (CMS) for static site generators

No all people are comfortable in working at the backend using VS Code or in writing mark-up files. Though it’s not that difficult, but if you have someone in your team who prefers a graphic user interface then we have that option too. 

Just like we have various page builders in Wordpress (e.g. Thrive, Elementor etc.), we have static CMS for static sites. 

Two most famous static CMS are:

### Forestry 

It’s the most famous CMS when it comes to static sites. You can write front-end code for many SSGs using Forestry, e.g. Hugo, Gatsby, Jekyll etc. 

Though not as feature rich as some of the page builders in Wordpress, it will allow your team to write markup files without touching a line of code. 

You can upload your site to Github and link it to your Forestry account. Now, you or your team members can work on the content files here and then save your work. Your work will get committed back to your Github without merge conflicts. 

And don’t worry, they do not save your content in their database. So, your content will remain yours, safe in your Github account. As there is no lock-in of content, you can stop using Forestry anytime you want. 

To read more about Forestry, you may check out their <a href="https://forestry.io/" target="_blank"> website </a>. 

### Netlifly CMS

If you are hosting your site on Netlify, then using Netlifly CMS maybe a good option for you. If you want to read more about it, you may check out their <a href="https://www.netlifycms.org/" target="_blank">website</a>.
